Idly


Description

Idli is a popular South Indian dish, typically served for breakfast.
It is made with fermented rice and urad dal (black gram), and is known for its soft and fluffy texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt, to taste
Step 1

Rinse the rice and urad dal separately. Soak the rice and fenugreek seeds together in a bowl, and the urad dal in another bowl. Soak for 6-8 hours or overnight.

Step 2

Grind the soaked urad dal into a smooth paste using water as needed. Similarly, grind the soaked rice mixture into a coarse batter. Combine both the rice and dal batters in a large bowl, and add salt to taste. Stir well to mix.

Step 3

Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours, or until it doubles in size and becomes slightly bubbly. Fermentation is key to making soft and fluffy idlis.

Step 4

Once the batter has fermented, grease the idli molds and pour the batter into the molds. Steam the idlis in a steamer or pressure cooker (without the pressure) for about 10-15 minutes, or until they are cooked through and a toothpick inserted comes out clean.

Step 5

Serve the idlis hot with chutney and sambar. Enjoy!
