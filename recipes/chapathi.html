Chapathi (Roti)


Description

Chapathi (also known as Roti) is a soft, flat, and unleavened bread made from whole wheat flour.
It is commonly served with curries, vegetables, and lentil dishes in many Indian households.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water, as needed
  • 2 teaspoons oil (optional, for softer chapathi)
Step 1

In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour and salt. Gradually add water and mix until the dough begins to come together.

Step 2

Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and soft. If the dough feels sticky, add a little more flour; if it feels too dry, add a few drops of water. Cover the dough and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.

Step 3

After resting, divide the dough into small equal-sized balls (about the size of a golf ball). Roll each ball into a smooth ball and flatten it slightly with your fingers.

Step 4

Using a rolling pin, roll each dough ball into a thin, round disc (about 6-8 inches in diameter) on a lightly floured surface.

Step 5

Heat a griddle (tawa) over medium heat. Once hot, place the rolled-out chapathi onto the griddle. Cook for about 30 seconds until you see bubbles forming on the surface. Flip the chapathi and cook for another 30 seconds.

Step 6

Press gently with a cloth or spatula to help it puff up. Flip again and cook for another 10-15 seconds. Once done, remove from the griddle and keep warm in a cloth or container.

Step 7

Serve the chapathi hot with curry, vegetables, or yogurt. Enjoy!
